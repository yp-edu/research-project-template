# Internal Review Checklist

When performing an internal review, consider:

1. **Clarity of problem statement**: Is the problem and motivation clearly stated?
2. **Method soundness**: Are assumptions explicit, and is the method technically sound?
3. **Baselines and ablations**: Are baselines reasonable, and are key design choices tested?
4. **Result support**: Do experiments support the claimed contributions?
5. **Reproducibility**: Are instructions and configs sufficient to re-run main results?
6. **Writing quality**: Is the paper readable, well structured, and free of major ambiguities?
7. **Limitations and ethics**: Are limitations and any relevant ethical considerations acknowledged?
