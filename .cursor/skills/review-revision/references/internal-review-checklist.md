# Internal Review Checklist

When performing an internal review, consider:

1. **Clarity of problem statement**: Is the problem and motivation clearly stated?
2. **Method soundness**: Are assumptions explicit, and is the method technically sound?
3. **Baselines and ablations**: Are baselines reasonable, and are key design choices tested?
4. **Result support**: Do experiments support the claimed contributions?
5. **Statistical validity**: Are variance, confidence intervals, or significance reported? Are sample sizes and number of runs sufficient? Would a skeptical reviewer accept the evidence?
6. **Scientific contribution and novelty**: Is the contribution substantive? Is novelty clearly stated and defended against prior work? Would a reviewer see this as incremental?
7. **Generalization**: Are limits of applicability stated? Is there evidence or discussion beyond the main setup (domains, scales, conditions)? Where might the method fail?
8. **Reproducibility**: Are instructions and configs sufficient to re-run main results?
9. **Writing quality**: Is the paper readable, well structured, and free of major ambiguities?
10. **Limitations and ethics**: Are limitations and any relevant ethical considerations acknowledged?
