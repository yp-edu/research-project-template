# Claims–Evidence Skill

## When to Use

- You are drafting or revising paper text and need to ensure every claim is backed by evidence.
- You are reviewing the paper for unsupported or fabricated claims.

## Instructions

- Use `references/check-rules.md` for the mapping rules.
- **When writing**: Before adding a claim (e.g. "our method improves over X"), identify the evidence: result file, config, table, or citation. If none exists, do not add the claim or mark it as TODO.
- **When reviewing**: For each substantive claim in the draft, list the corresponding evidence (path or citation). Flag any claim with no evidence.
- **Never fabricate**: Do not invent numbers, tables, or figures. Ask the user for real outputs or suggest running the experiment first.
