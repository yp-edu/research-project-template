# Background Writing Skill

## When to Use

- You have grouped literature notes (from **literature-notes**) and need to draft background or related-work content for the paper.
- The user wants bullets or prose for `latex/sections/2-background.tex` or `latex/sections/6-related-work.tex`.

## Instructions

- Use `references/guidelines.md` for tone and structure.
- **Input**: Theme-grouped notes (papers per theme). **Output**: Bullets or short paragraphs with citations, faithful to the notes.
- Do not invent claims; only translate and lightly synthesize what is in the notes.
- Suggest where to insert the content (e.g. "Background §2.2", "Related work §6.1"). Prefer small, reviewable edits when changing LaTeX.
